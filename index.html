IT2 Discrete Math Master Guide

Core Dictionary Rules

A Predicate represents the property, trait, or relationship being described. Use Uppercase Letters.

S(x) = "is a suspect"

A Variable is a placeholder for a general template. Use lowercase letters from the end of the alphabet.

x (e.g., "For all x...")

A Constant is a specific subject (person, place, thing). Use lowercase letters from the start or middle of the alphabet.

m = Mary

The Quantifiers

Universal Quantifier

∀

Keywords: All, every, each, any, for all

Translation Tip:

Usually paired with the conditional (→).

"All rats are grey"

∀x [R(x) → G(x)]

Existential Quantifier

∃

Keywords: Some, there exists, at least one

Translation Tip:

Usually paired with logical AND (∧).

"Some rats are grey"

∃x [R(x) ∧ G(x)]

Step-by-Step Translation

Example Sentence: "Every student who studies hard will pass."

1

Create the Dictionary

Identify the properties and assign uppercase letters.

S(x): x is a student
H(x): x studies hard
P(x): x will pass
2

Identify the Quantifier

Look for keywords like "Every", "All", "Some", "Nobody".

Keyword is "Every". We use the Universal Quantifier: ∀x
3

Determine Connectives & Groups

Universals (∀) pair with IF-THEN (→). Existentials (∃) pair with AND (∧).

Condition (If): S(x) ∧ H(x) "is a student AND studies hard"
Result (Then): → P(x) "will pass"
4

Assemble the Final Formula

Combine the quantifier, the condition, and the result together in brackets.

∀x [(S(x) ∧ H(x)) → P(x)]
