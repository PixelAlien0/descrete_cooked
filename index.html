Core Dictionary Rules

1. Predicates

Represents the property, trait, or relationship. Use Uppercase Letters.

T(x) = "is Tall"
L(x,y) = "Loves"

2. Variables

Placeholders for general templates. Use lowercase from the end of alphabet.

x, y, z

3. Constants

Specific subjects (people, things). Use lowercase from start/middle.

m = Mary
b = Bill

The Quantifiers

Universal Quantifier

∀

Keywords: All, every, each, any, for all

Translation Tip:

Usually paired with the conditional (→).

"All rats are grey"

∀x [R(x) → G(x)]

Existential Quantifier

∃

Keywords: Some, there exists, at least one

Translation Tip:

Usually paired with logical AND (∧).

"Some rats are grey"

∃x [R(x) ∧ G(x)]
